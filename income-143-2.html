Section 143(2) — Scrutiny Notice

A scrutiny notice under Section 143(2) is issued when the Assessing Officer intends to examine the return more thoroughly. It often requests documents and explanations and may lead to adjustments or scrutiny assessment.

Typical triggers

  • Large/unexplained transactions, abnormal expenses or round-tripping.
  • Mismatches between reported figures and third-party data (TDS, bank reporting).
  • Related-party transactions or transfer pricing concerns.

Documents & checklist

  • Complete books of account and supporting vouchers
  • Bank statements, ledger reconciliations
  • Invoices, contracts, agreements, and payment proofs
  • Compliances like TDS challans, advance tax payments

Reply strategy

  1. Create a clear index of annexures matching the AO’s queries.
  2. Provide reconciliations that show step-by-step how claimed figures arise.
  3. If a claim is weak on evidence, prepare fallback explanations and admissions where required to limit exposure.
  4. Where legal interpretation is involved, reference authoritative judgments or circulars briefly in the reply (avoid long legal essays — attach separate note).

Sample reply (editable)

To
The Assessing Officer,
[Address]

Subject: Reply to notice under Section 143(2) dated [dd-mm-yyyy] – PAN [●], AY [●]

Respected Sir/Madam,

1. We refer to your notice dated [date] seeking documents/explanations in respect of PAN [●] for AY [●]. Our response below addresses each point raised:

2. Point 1 (Sales discrepancy): Please refer to Annexure A — sales reconciliation and supporting invoices.

3. Point 2 (High expenses): Annexure B contains expense ledgers, payment receipts and third-party confirmations supporting the payments.

4. Point 3 (TDS mismatch): Annexure C contains TDS certificates and Form 26AS reconciliation.

5. We request that the AO kindly consider the attached documents. We remain available for any further clarification or personal hearing.

Yours faithfully,
[Name / Authorised Signatory]
