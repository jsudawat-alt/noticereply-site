Section 61 — Scrutiny of Returns

Section 61 authorises scrutiny of returns to verify the correctness of returns, to detect tax evasion, and to ensure proper discharge of tax liabilities. Scrutiny notices request documents and reconciliations.

Why the department issues a Section 61 notice

  • Mismatch between reported supplies and third-party data.
  • Unusual spikes or low tax liability compared to industry norms.
  • Discrepancies between GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B or ITC records.

Checklist — documents to prepare

  • GSTR-1, GSTR-3B and reconciliation statements for the period(s)
  • Sales invoices and supporting delivery documents
  • Payment challans and bank statements
  • Purchase register and ITC claim supporting invoices

Step-by-step reply process

  1. Read the notice carefully and map each query to supporting documents.
  2. Prepare a reconciliation table (GSTR-1 vs GSTR-3B vs books) and highlight reasons for differences.
  3. Attach supporting invoices and challans as Annexures, indexed to points in your reply.
  4. Submit reply through the GST portal or as instructed and keep acknowledgment reference for records.

Practical drafting tips

  • Present the reconciliation concisely — a clear table resolves many queries without further follow-up.
  • If there is an inadvertent omission, admit the factual shortfall and show corrective steps (amend return, pay tax/interest).
  • Where differences are technical (e.g., time-of-supply), include brief legal / rule references in a supporting note.

Sample reply (editable)

To
The Proper Officer,
[GST Office Address]

Subject: Reply to notice under Section 61 dated [dd-mm-yyyy] — GSTIN [●]

Respected Sir/Madam,

1. We refer to the notice dated [date] seeking clarifications and documents for the period [mm/yyyy] to [mm/yyyy]. Please find enclosed reconciliations and supporting documents as follows:

   - Annexure A: GSTR-1 vs GSTR-3B reconciliation.
   - Annexure B: Sales invoices (Invoice Nos. … to …).
   - Annexure C: Bank payment proofs and challans.

2. The differences highlighted in the reconciliation are due to [brief reason — reporting timing / credit notes / system lag]. Copies of corrective filings (where applicable) are included.

3. We request the officer to consider the attached reconciliation and documents. We remain available for any further clarifications.

Yours faithfully,
[Authorised Signatory]
