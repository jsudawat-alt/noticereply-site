Section 156 — Demand Notice

A Section 156 notice demands payment of tax, interest, penalty or any sum due under an assessment order. It follows a demand calculation and requires prompt attention to avoid recovery action.

Immediate checks

  • Confirm the demand figure shown in the notice and cross-check with your records and assessment orders.
  • Check whether any previous payments/adjustments reduce the demand.
  • Identify if appeal or rectification is pending which may impact the demand.

Steps to respond

  1. Verify calculations and reconcile with assessment order and earlier payments.
  2. If demand is correct, arrange payment immediately (note payment challan details in reply).
  3. If demand is disputed, file rectification/appeal as appropriate and inform the AO with reasons and proof of dispute.
  4. Keep proof of payment or proof of filing appeal / rectification and include references in reply.

Sample reply (editable)

To
The Assessing Officer,
[Address]

Subject: Reply to notice under Section 156 dated [dd-mm-yyyy] – PAN [●], AY [●]

Respected Sir/Madam,

1. We acknowledge receipt of the demand notice dated [date] showing a demand of Rs. [●]. We have verified the ledger and confirm that payment of Rs. [●] was made on [date] challan no. [●] (copy enclosed).

2. [If payment pending] We will make the payment of Rs. [●] within the time allowed and enclose the payment challan details.

3. [If disputed] We have filed an appeal / rectification on [date] and request that recovery action be stayed until the appeal is decided. Enclosed: copy of appeal / rectification reference.

Yours faithfully,
[Name / Authorised Signatory]

If the demand calculation is complex and needs verification, ask NOTICEREPLY for a rapid ledger review.
