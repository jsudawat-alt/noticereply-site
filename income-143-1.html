Income Tax Notice — Section 143(1) (Intimation)

Section 143(1) intimations are automated computations or adjustments performed by the Income Tax Department. This page explains common triggers, documents to check, and a ready-to-edit reply draft you can use.

Summary

143(1) intimations are generally for arithmetic corrections, incorrect or missing TDS reporting, mismatch in gross receipts, or computational changes. First step is to compare the department’s computation with your filed return and your records.

Checklist (documents to gather)

  • Filed return (XML / PDF)
  • Bank statements for the relevant year
  • TDS certificates (Form 16 / 16A / 26AS)
  • Sales / purchase registers or reconciliation schedules
  • Any supporting invoices / payment receipts

Practical approach

  1. Reconcile the department’s figures vs your books.
  2. If it’s a genuine mismatch, prepare reconciliation table and attach supporting docs.
  3. If the intimation is incorrect, prepare a short factual reply with annexures.
  4. File rectification or respond to the e-portal as required within time limits.

Sample reply (editable)

To
The Assessing Officer,
[Address]

Subject: Reply to Intimation under Section 143(1) for AY [●]

Respected Sir/Madam,

1. We refer to the intimation dated [date] under section 143(1) in respect of PAN [●] for Assessment Year [●].

2. The department’s computation shows adjustments on account of [briefly state]. We have reconciled the return filed with our books and enclose the reconciliation statement and supporting documents (Annexures A to D).

3. [If agreement] We accept the adjustment and request guidance for payment / rectification as required.

   [Or If disagreement] We respectfully submit that the department’s computation is based on [mention reason], and enclose supporting documents (Annexures A–D) showing the correct position.

4. We request that the intimation be reviewed in light of the enclosed documents.

Yours faithfully,
[Taxpayer/Authorised Signatory]
