Section 148 — Reassessment (Notice of Income Escaping Assessment)

A notice under Section 148 is issued when the Assessing Officer believes that income has escaped assessment. This is a serious notice and often requires careful legal and factual analysis before replying or filing returns.

Key considerations

  • Check limitation period — reassessments are governed by statutory time limits which vary by case.
  • Confirm the reason for reopening (new information, failure to disclose material facts, etc.).
  • Consider whether reassessment is valid — grounds for reopening must be recorded by AO.

Checklist (documents to prepare)

  • Original return and computation
  • Documents or evidence relied upon by AO (attach if available)
  • Correspondence and past assessment orders
  • Reconciliations and supporting invoices

Reply strategy

  1. Do not admit facts hastily — prepare a cautious factual reply acknowledging receipt and requesting specifics if not provided.
  2. Ask for the reasons recorded (if not already supplied) and records relied upon.
  3. If the reopening appears invalid (time-barred or no reasons), prepare a reasoned legal response citing precedent and statutory limits.
  4. Where taxable error exists, reconcile quantum and propose settlement or admission with calculations and payment details.

Sample reply (editable)

To
The Assessing Officer,
[Address]

Subject: Reply to notice under Section 148 dated [dd-mm-yyyy] – PAN [●], AY [●]

Respected Sir/Madam,

1. We acknowledge receipt of your notice dated [date] under Section 148. The notice does not specify the precise material or information relied upon for reopening. Kindly provide the reasons recorded and any records relied upon.

2. In the meanwhile, we enclose a brief factual note and reconciliations for the items reported in the original return (Annexures A–C). If required, we will file the return under Section 148A along with supporting records once the reasons are furnished.

3. We respectfully reserve the right to make detailed submissions after examining the reasons recorded.

Yours faithfully,
[Name / Authorised Signatory]
