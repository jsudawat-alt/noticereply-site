Section 73 — Demand (No Fraud)

Section 73 deals with recovery of tax, interest and penalty where demand arises but there is no allegation of fraud or wilful misstatement. Notices require calculation details and evidence supporting any claimed adjustments.

Key points to verify

  • Exact period and tax head under which demand is alleged.
  • Whether tax has already been paid or credited.
  • Whether interest and penalty are correctly computed.

Checklist

  • Reconciled GSTR returns and books for the period
  • Payment challans and bank statements
  • Any amended returns or credit notes issued

Reply strategy

  1. Recompute the demand as per department’s basis and prepare your own computation showing differences (if any).
  2. If demand is payable, supply challan details and pay promptly to avoid recovery procedures.
  3. If disputed, attach documentary evidence and legal/technical note explaining your stand; propose payment of admitted tax and contest balance if needed.

Sample reply (editable)

To
The Proper Officer,
[GST Office Address]

Subject: Reply to notice under Section 73 dated [dd-mm-yyyy] — GSTIN [●]

Respected Sir/Madam,

1. We refer to the notice dated [date] alleging demand of Rs. [●] for the period [mm/yyyy]. We enclose our computation and reconciliations (Annexure A) demonstrating differences between department figures and our books.

2. We confirm that tax challan no. [●] dated [date] for Rs. [●] is credited (copy enclosed). For the balance (if any), we propose [payment / clarification] and request acceptance of enclosed documents.

3. We respectfully request the officer to consider the reconciliation and supporting documents.

Yours faithfully,
[Authorised Signatory]
