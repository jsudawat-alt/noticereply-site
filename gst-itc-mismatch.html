ITC Mismatch — Reconciliation (GSTR-2A/2B vs GSTR-3B)

ITC mismatch notices arise when there is a difference between supplier-reported entries (GSTR-2A/2B) and recipient claims in GSTR-3B. Correct reconciliation is essential to avoid disallowance or demands.

Checklist

  • GSTR-2A / 2B for the period
  • GSTR-3B and books of account
  • Purchase invoices, GRNs, payment proofs
  • Supplier confirmations for missing invoices (if any)

Reconciliation approach

  1. Download GSTR-2A/2B and prepare a table comparing each invoice to recipient’s books.
  2. Highlight invoices present in supplier data but not claimed by recipient (or vice versa) and provide reasons (timing, credit notes, returns).
  3. Obtain supplier confirmations where invoices are incorrectly reported or missing.
  4. Where recipient claim is incorrect, reverse or correct the ITC in subsequent returns and show corrective filings in reply.

Sample reply (editable)

To
The Proper Officer,
[GST Office Address]

Subject: Reply to notice regarding ITC mismatch — GSTIN [●]

Respected Sir/Madam,

1. We refer to the notice dated [date] citing differences between GSTR-2A/2B and our GSTR-3B for the period [mm/yyyy]. We enclose a reconciliation (Annexure A) and supplier confirmations where applicable.

2. The reconciliation shows:
   - Invoices correctly reported and claimed: Annexure B.
   - Supplier-reported invoices not claimed due to timing: Annexure C (supplier confirmations enclosed).
   - Where ITC was incorrectly claimed, corrective filings (GSTR-3B amendment) were filed on [date] (proof enclosed).

3. We request the officer to consider the reconciliation and accept the corrective actions taken.

Yours faithfully,
[Authorised Signatory]
