Section 74 — Demand (With Fraud)

Section 74 applies where the tax authority alleges fraud, wilful misstatement, or suppression of facts. These notices often carry higher penalties and require a careful combination of factual and legal response backed by evidence.

Immediate actions

  • Treat the notice as high-priority — collect all records immediately.
  • Identify transactions alleged to be fraudulent and trace documentary flow (invoices, contracts, payments).
  • Consider engaging counsel — these matters may proceed to prosecution in severe cases.

Checklist

  • Invoices, contracts, delivery proofs, E-way bills
  • Bank records, payment receipts and reconciliation
  • Correspondence with suppliers/customers proving genuineness of transactions

Drafting & strategy

  1. Provide a fact-by-fact rebuttal with documentary evidence for each allegation.
  2. Include third-party confirmations where possible (supplier declarations, delivery acknowledgements).
  3. For complex legal issues, include a short legal note referencing judgments and statutory provisions.
  4. Be careful with admissions — avoid language that could be construed as conceding fraud.

Sample reply (editable)

To
The Proper Officer,
[GST Office Address]

Subject: Reply to notice under Section 74 dated [dd-mm-yyyy] — GSTIN [●]

Respected Sir/Madam,

1. We refer to the notice dated [date] alleging suppression/fraud in respect of GSTIN [●]. We strongly deny any fraudulent intent and submit the following with documentary evidence:

   - Annexure A: Original invoices and delivery proofs.
   - Annexure B: Bank payment confirmations.
   - Annexure C: Supplier declarations confirming transactions.

2. The transactions are genuine and supported by the enclosed documents. We invite the officer to examine the annexures and request an opportunity for personal hearing before any demand/penalty is confirmed.

3. We reserve our right to make detailed submissions following examination of the records relied upon by the department.

Yours faithfully,
[Authorised Signatory]
